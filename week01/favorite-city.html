Lagos

Lagos, popularly known as Èkó in the Yoruba language, is the largest city in Nigeria and one of the most populous, fast-paced megacities in the world. Located on the southwestern coast of the country along the Bight of Benin, it is a dynamic metropolis that serves as the undisputed cultural, economic, and technological heartbeat of West Africa.

Geography and Landscape

Lagos is uniquely defined by its geography, stretching across a complex network of islands, sandbars, and lagoons that open up into the Atlantic Ocean. The city is structurally divided into two major regions: The Islands: This includes Lagos Island, Victoria Island, and Ikoyi. Historically the nucleus of the city, the islands act as the upscale financial districts, featuring high-rise corporate headquarters, luxury residential areas, and vibrant nightlife. The Mainland: Connected to the islands by massive infrastructure like the Third Mainland Bridge, the Mainland is home to the vast majority of the city's population. It houses the state capital (Ikeja), key transport hubs, industrial zones, and sprawling residential neighborhoods.
